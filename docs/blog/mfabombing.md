# MFA Bombing

## Not a Real Bomb, but Dangerous Nevertheless!

In the rapidly evolving landscape of cybersecurity, the prevalence of cyberattacks continues to escalate, with attackers employing increasingly sophisticated methods to compromise sensitive information. One such prominent method is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) bombing, a technique that exploits users’ trust of MFA prompts. So, let’s get started and explore the concept of MFA bombing, its potential consequences, and emphasize the critical importance of remaining vigilant and discerning when confronted with MFA prompts.

## Understanding MFA Bombing

As the name implies, MFA typically involves two or more factors required to authenticate, usually something you know (i.e., your password), and something you have (i.e., your mobile device) or something you are (i.e., your biometrics).  

**MFA Bombing** – also known as MFA Fatigue Attack or MFA Spamming – **exploits this process by bombarding a user with seemingly legitimate MFA prompts, tricking them into approving access requests that they did not initiate.**

Attackers leverage various tactics to carry out MFA bombing, including phishing emails, malicious websites, smishing & vishing (aka phishing respectively through text or phone call), and compromised mobile applications. By creating a false sense of urgency, attackers manipulate users into hastily approving MFA prompts without carefully verifying the authenticity of the request. MFA is undeniably the guarantee for success in any high-profile cyber attack. When carried out properly, attacks on MFA have a high probability of success to the unsuspecting user. The attackers are getting progressively better at tricking employees into approving illegitimate access requests.  Namely, they’re using our fatigue and laziness against us. Cybercriminals are pelting employees with MFA notifications over and over, and over again, until something gives.  

## The consequences of MFA Bombing

The consequences of falling victim to MFA bombing can be severe. Unauthorized access to sensitive accounts, confidential data breaches, and financial losses are just a few potential outcomes. Once an attacker gains access to an account, they may exploit it for further attacks, conduct identity theft, or even compromise an entire network.

## What to do if you think you're being targeted by an MFA Bombing?

**Don’t approve it!:** Never approve an MFA notification unless you are the person who triggered it. Avoid hasty approvals and take the time to carefully review and understand any MFA prompt. Avoid rushing through the process, especially if the request appears unexpected or out of context.

**Double-Check Communication Channels:** Be wary of MFA prompts received via email, SMS, or other communication channels. Legitimate authentication requests are typically sent through the official application or platform.

**Verify the Source:** Before approving any MFA prompt, users should verify the source of the request. Legitimate MFA prompts will only be triggered by actions initiated by the user, such as attempting to log in or make account changes.

If it is happening to **your work account**, immediately contact Security Architect via SOS/ so we can fully stop the potential attack.


Finally, as cyber threats evolve, we must remain vigilant and adapt to new attack vectors like MFA bombing. By questioning the legitimacy of MFA prompts and taking the time to verify their authenticity, we can significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access and protect our sensitive information and Ubisoft from external threats.
